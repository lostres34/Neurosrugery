Patient Education

This guide provides essential information about common neurological conditions, surgical treatments, and follow-up care instructions.

1. Brain Tumors

Includes gliomas, meningiomas, pituitary tumors, etc. Treatment options may involve surgical removal, radiation therapy, or chemotherapy.

Follow-up: Regular imaging (MRI/CT), hormone checks (for pituitary), and oncologist consultations.

2. Hydrocephalus

A buildup of cerebrospinal fluid in the brain, often treated with a ventriculoperitoneal (VP) shunt or endoscopic third ventriculostomy (ETV).

Follow-up: Monitoring for symptoms of increased intracranial pressure, regular imaging, and shunt function checks.

3. Herniated Disc

Causes nerve root compression resulting in pain or numbness. Treated with conservative management or discectomy surgery.

Follow-up: Physiotherapy, gradual return to activity, posture education, and surgical site healing checks.

4. Spinal Stenosis

Narrowing of spinal canal, leading to nerve compression. Treated with decompression surgery (laminectomy) or spinal fusion.

Follow-up: Physical rehabilitation, pain management, and surgical wound care.

5. Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI)

Requires close monitoring, surgery for hematoma evacuation, and neurocritical care.

Follow-up: Cognitive rehabilitation, neurological exams, and family education on long-term care needs.

6. Trigeminal Neuralgia

Characterized by facial nerve pain. Treatment includes medication, microvascular decompression, or radiosurgery.

Follow-up: Medication adjustments and regular neurology consults.

7. Pituitary Disorders

Managed by transsphenoidal surgery and endocrinological follow-up.

Follow-up: Hormone replacement, vision checks, and repeat MRIs.
